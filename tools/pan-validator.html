PAN Card Number Validator

Verify if a PAN (Permanent Account Number) is in the correct 10-character format as issued by the Income Tax Department of India. This tool decodes the entity type, holder's surname initial, and more — all in your browser, with no data transmitted.

Uppercase letters and numbers only. 10 characters total.

Note: This tool validates the format and structure only. It does NOT verify if the PAN is active or registered with the Income Tax Department.

Frequently Asked Questions

A PAN is always 10 characters: the first 5 are uppercase letters, the next 4 are digits, and the last is an uppercase letter. For example: ABCDE1234F. The 4th character specifically indicates what type of taxpayer holds this PAN.

Both companies and individuals have PAN — the difference is in the 4th character. An individual's PAN has 'P' at position 4, while a company has 'C'. This allows the Income Tax Department to categorise taxpayers. Both follow the exact same 10-character format.
