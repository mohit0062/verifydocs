What is an Aadhaar Number?

Aadhaar is a 12-digit unique identification number issued by the Unique Identification Authority of India (UIDAI) to Indian residents. It serves as proof of identity and address across India. As of 2024, over 1.3 billion Aadhaar numbers have been issued.

How does this validator work?

Our validator applies the Verhoeff algorithm — a checksum formula invented by Dutch mathematician Jacobus Verhoeff in 1969. UIDAI uses this algorithm to embed a mathematical check in the last digit of every Aadhaar number. Any valid Aadhaar number, when processed through this algorithm, will produce a checksum result of 0. If even a single digit is mistyped, the checksum will fail.

What errors does Verhoeff detect?

  • Single digit substitution (e.g., typing 5 instead of 3)
  • Transposition of adjacent digits (e.g., 12 → 21)
  • Twin errors (e.g., 11 → 22)
  • Jump transpositions (e.g., 132 → 231)
