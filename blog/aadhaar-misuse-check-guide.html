Aadhaar Security: How to Check if Your Aadhaar is Being Misused

In the digital age, your Aadhaar card is more than just an ID—it's a gateway to your financial and personal ecosystem. With the increasing number of services linked to Aadhaar, the risk of identity theft and misuse has also grown. But did you know that UIDAI provides tools to monitor exactly when and where your Aadhaar was used?

This guide will show you how to track your Aadhaar authentication history and take control of your digital identity.

What is Aadhaar Authentication History?

Every time you use your Aadhaar for a SIM card, a bank account, or a government subsidy, an authentication request is sent to the UIDAI servers. UIDAI maintains a detailed log of these requests for the last six months. You can check these logs to see if there are any unauthorized attempts to use your data.

How to Check Your History (Step-by-Step)

  1. Visit the MyAadhaar Portal: Go to the official UIDAI website (myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in) and log in using your Aadhaar number and the OTP sent to your registered mobile number.
  2. Find 'Authentication History': Once logged in, look for the 'Authentication History' tab in the dashboard.
  3. Select Date Range: You can select a date range (up to 6 months) to view the logs.
  4. Review the Logs: The report will show the type of authentication (Biometric, Demographic, OTP), the date, time, and the name of the 'Authentication User Agency' (AUA) that requested the data.

What to Do if You Find Suspicious Activity?

If you see an entry from an agency you don't recognize or at a time you didn't use your Aadhaar, don't panic. Follow these steps:

  • Lock Your Biometrics: Use the 'Lock/Unlock Biometrics' feature on the MyAadhaar portal. This prevents anyone from using your fingerprint or iris for authentication.
  • Call 1947: This is the UIDAI toll-free helpline. Report the unauthorized usage immediately.
  • File a Cyber Complaint: Visit cybercrime.gov.in to file an official report if you suspect financial fraud.

Pro-Tip: Use Virtual ID (VID)

Instead of sharing your actual 12-digit Aadhaar number everywhere, generate a 16-digit Virtual ID (VID) from the UIDAI website. You can use this VID for most authentications, and it protects your original Aadhaar number from being stored in third-party databases.

Conclusion

Your Aadhaar is your digital soul in India. Spend 5 minutes every month checking your authentication history. It's a small habit that can prevent massive headaches in the future.
