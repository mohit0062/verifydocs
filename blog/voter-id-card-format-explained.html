The Voter ID card, officially known as the Electors Photo Identity Card (EPIC), is a crucial document for Indian citizens. It not only allows you to vote but also serves as a valid proof of identity and address. But have you ever looked closely at the 10-character number on your card?

This number, known as the EPIC number, has a specific format that encodes information about where it was issued. In this guide, we'll break down the structure of the Voter ID card number.

What is an EPIC Number?

The EPIC number is the unique identification number assigned to every registered voter in India by the Election Commission of India (ECI). Since the early 2000s, the ECI has standardized this number into a 10-character alphanumeric format.

Voter ID (EPIC) Number Structure

A modern Voter ID number follows this structure: ABC1234567

The First 3 Characters: State Code

The first three characters are uppercase English letters. These usually represent a specific state or a region within the state. For example:

  • DL/ — Often used for Delhi (in older formats)
  • UP/ — Often used for Uttar Pradesh (in older formats)
  • WB/ — Often used for West Bengal (in older formats)

In the newer 10-character alphanumeric series, these three letters are assigned to specific areas to ensure uniqueness across the country. Common prefixes include ZNC, HRY, MTL, etc.

The Last 7 Characters: Serial Number

The remaining seven characters are numeric digits. This is a unique serial number assigned to the voter within that regional series. This combination ensures that no two citizens have the same EPIC number.

Old vs. New Voter ID Formats

If you have an very old Voter ID card, the number might look different. Older formats were often purely numeric or had different lengths (e.g., 123456 or S01/001/001234). However, most of these have now been converted to the standard 10-character format in the National Elector's Service Portal (NVSP).

How to Verify Your Voter ID Number?

  1. Check the physical card: The EPIC number is usually printed at the top or above your photograph.
  2. Use the NVSP Portal: Visit voters.eci.gov.in and use the "Search in Electoral Roll" feature.
  3. Voter Helpline App: Download the official ECI app to verify your details on the go.

Is Voter ID Proof of Citizenship?

While the Voter ID card is issued to citizens of India for the purpose of voting, it is primarily a proof of enrollment in the electoral roll. For official citizenship matters, documents like a Passport or Birth Certificate are often given higher weightage, although a Voter ID is accepted as a primary ID for almost all local services.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if my Voter ID number is only 8 digits?

You likely have an older card. You can visit the NVSP portal to find your updated 10-character EPIC number linked to your registration.

Can I change my EPIC number?

No, your EPIC number remains the same even if you move to a new address or state. You only need to update your address in the electoral roll (Form 8).
