UPI ID Format Explained — Understanding VPA Structure

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) has revolutionized how we transfer money in India. But when you're typing in a UPI ID, have you ever wondered about its structure? Understanding the UPI ID format can help you spot errors and avoid sending money to the wrong person.

A UPI ID, also known as a Virtual Payment Address (VPA), is a unique identifier that links to your bank account without exposing sensitive details like your account number or IFSC code.

The Anatomy of a UPI ID

A typical UPI ID follows a simple structure: username@bankname

PartWhat it representsExample
Handle/UsernameYour unique name or phone number9876543210
SeparatorThe '@' symbol@
PSP SuffixThe bank or app identifierokhdfcbank

The Handle (Username)

The part before the '@' is your unique handle. Depending on the app you use, this can be:

  • Phone Number: Most popular apps like Google Pay or PhonePe use your phone number by default.
  • Name/Custom text: You can often create a custom handle like rahul.kumar.
  • Email: Some banking apps allow using part of your email address.

The Suffix (Bank Extension)

The part after the '@' is the Payment Service Provider (PSP) identifier. Each app or bank has its own specific suffixes. Some common examples include:

Google Pay (GPay)

@okaxis, @okhdfcbank, @oksbi, @okicici

PhonePe

@ybl, @ibl, @axl

Paytm

@paytm

BHIM App

@upi

Common UPI Format Rules

To be valid, a UPI ID must follow these technical rules:

  • It cannot start or end with a special character.
  • It must contain exactly one '@' symbol.
  • Characters allowed: a-z, 0-9, . , - , _
  • It should not contain spaces.

How to Find Your UPI ID?

Open your payment app (PhonePe, GPay, Paytm) and tap on your profile picture or the "My UPI ID" section. You will see your unique VPA listed there. You can also generate multiple UPI IDs for the same bank account in most apps.

Is it safe to share your UPI ID?

Yes, sharing your UPI ID is generally safe as it only allows people to send you money. However, never enter your UPI PIN unless you are making a payment. No one needs your PIN to send you money. Beware of "Collect Requests" from unknown IDs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I have multiple UPI IDs?

Yes, you can have multiple UPI IDs across different apps, all linked to the same or different bank accounts.

What happens if I enter the wrong UPI ID?

If the format is wrong, the app will show an error. If the format is correct but the ID belongs to someone else, the money will be sent to them. Always verify the name before confirming.
