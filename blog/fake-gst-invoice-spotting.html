GST Invoice Verification: How to Spot Fake GST Numbers Online

As a business owner or a consumer, you might have encountered invoices with GST charges added to the total. But are you sure the merchant is actually registered for GST? Some unscrupulous business owners use fake GST numbers to collect extra money from customers without ever paying it to the government.

Verifying a GSTIN is quick, free, and can save you from being a victim of tax fraud.

The Structure of a GSTIN

Before using a tool, you can often spot a fake just by looking at the format. A valid GSTIN (Goods and Services Tax Identification Number) is 15 characters long:

  • First 2 digits: State Code (e.g., 08 for Rajasthan, 27 for Maharashtra).
  • Next 10 characters: The PAN of the business entity.
  • 13th character: Entity number (usually 1 or 2).
  • 14th character: The letter 'Z' by default.
  • 15th character: Check digit (can be a number or letter).

How to Verify Online

Once you have the GSTIN from the invoice, follow these steps:

  1. Official Portal: Go to the GST portal (gst.gov.in) and click on 'Search Taxpayer' > 'Search by GSTIN/UIN'.
  2. Enter Details: Enter the GSTIN and the captcha code.
  3. Check Status: The portal will show the legal name of the business, the trade name, and most importantly, the Registration Status (Active, Suspended, or Cancelled).

Red Flags to Watch Out For

Keep an eye out for these common tactics used in fake invoices:

  • Handwritten GSTIN: While not always illegal, a handwritten GSTIN on a printed invoice is a major red flag.
  • Missing PAN link: If the 3rd to 12th characters don't match the PAN of the business mentioned on the shop board, it's likely fake.
  • Expired Status: If the portal shows 'Cancelled', the business cannot legally collect GST from you.

Conclusion

Don't pay GST blindly. A quick 1-minute check on the GST portal ensures that your hard-earned money is actually going to the nation's development rather than into a fraudster's pocket.
