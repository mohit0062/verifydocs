The Rise of UPI Scams: 7 Ways to Protect Your Bank Account

UPI has revolutionized payments in India, making it as easy as sending a WhatsApp message. However, this ease of use has also attracted scammers. From fake QR codes to 'money received' scams, digital thieves are constantly finding new ways to drain bank accounts.

Staying safe doesn't require technical expertise—just a healthy dose of skepticism and a few good habits.

1. You NEVER need a PIN to Receive Money

This is the most common scam. A fraudster will send you a 'Collect Request' and tell you to enter your PIN to receive money. Remember: PIN is only required to SEND money. If an app asks for your PIN, money is leaving your account, not entering it.

2. Avoid Scanning QR Codes for Payments You're Receiving

Similar to the PIN scam, fraudsters often send QR codes over WhatsApp claiming it's for a payment they're sending to you. Scanning a QR code and entering your PIN is an authorization to pay, not to get paid.

3. Verify the Name Before Hitting 'Pay'

When you scan a QR code at a shop or enter a VPA (UPI ID), your app will show the registered name of the recipient. Always double-check if the name matches the person or business you intend to pay. Scammers often use similar-sounding names to confuse victims.

4. Never Share Your Screen During a Call

Scammers posing as 'Bank Support' or 'KYC Officers' may ask you to download screen-sharing apps like AnyDesk or TeamViewer. Once they see your screen, they can see your OTPs and PINs as you type them. No legitimate bank will ever ask you to share your screen.

5. Set Daily Transaction Limits

Most UPI apps and banks allow you to set a daily limit for transactions. Setting a reasonable limit (e.g., ₹5,000 or ₹10,000) can minimize the damage if your account is ever compromised.

6. Be Wary of 'High-Value' OLX/Marketplace Deals

Scammers often post too-good-to-be-true deals on marketplaces. They'll insist on paying via UPI advance or sending you a 'Collect Request' to 'block the item'. Always deal in person for such transactions if possible.

7. Update Your UPI App Regularly

Apps like PhonePe, Google Pay, and Paytm frequently release security patches. Ensure your app is always updated to the latest version to benefit from the newest fraud detection algorithms.

Conclusion

Digital safety is a journey, not a destination. By following these 7 simple rules, you can enjoy the convenience of UPI without the constant fear of losing your hard-earned money.
