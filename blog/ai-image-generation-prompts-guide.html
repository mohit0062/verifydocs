AI Image Generation: The Ultimate Prompts Guide

Generating stunning images with AI isn't magic—it's about speaking the right language. If you type "a cool car" into Midjourney or DALL-E, you'll get a generic image. If you type "A vintage 1969 Mustang driving through neon-lit Tokyo streets at midnight, cinematic lighting, 8k resolution, photorealistic, shot on 35mm lens," you will get a masterpiece.

The Anatomy of a Perfect Prompt

A highly effective AI image prompt usually follows a specific structure:

  • Subject: What is the main focus? Be specific (e.g., "A golden retriever wearing a spacesuit").
  • Medium/Style: How is it made? (e.g., Oil painting, 3D render, watercolor, cinematic photograph).
  • Lighting: Lighting defines the mood. (e.g., Golden hour, neon lighting, volumetric fog, studio lighting).
  • Artist Reference: AI understands artistic styles. (e.g., "in the style of Studio Ghibli" or "by Vincent van Gogh").
  • Quality Modifiers: Words that push the AI to generate higher detail (e.g., 8k, highly detailed, Unreal Engine 5, octane render).

Prompting Differences: Midjourney vs. DALL-E

Not all AI models are the same. DALL-E 3 (built into ChatGPT) understands conversational English very well. You can write a paragraph explaining exactly what you want, and it will follow instructions closely.

Midjourney, on the other hand, responds better to comma-separated keywords and visual adjectives. It prioritizes aesthetics over strict adherence to the prompt.

Fixing Common Problems

If your AI image looks wrong, try these fixes:

  • Weird Hands/Faces: This is a common AI flaw. Add modifiers like "perfect anatomy, highly detailed face." Or frame the shot differently (e.g., "portrait shot from chest up").
  • Image is too cartoonish: If you want realism, explicitly state it. Use terms like "photorealistic, actual photograph, RAW image, f/1.8 aperture."
  • Too much clutter: If the AI is adding things you don't want, use negative prompts. For example, in Midjourney, adding `--no trees` ensures no trees appear in the image.

Conclusion

Prompt engineering is a skill that takes practice. Keep tweaking your keywords, study the prompts of images you admire, and build your own "swipe file" of effective lighting and style modifiers.
